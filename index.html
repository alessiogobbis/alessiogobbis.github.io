Areas of Expertise and the AG+AI Method

Dr. Alessio Gobbis’ practice has evolved beyond traditional financial advisory. After a solid background in managing SICAVs, mutual funds, and unit-linked products — now considered foundational but insufficient to excel — he has steered his approach toward a highly technical advisory service, grounded in data, algorithms, and quantitative forecasting powered by artificial intelligence.

Artificial intelligence here does not replace human expertise but rather enhances it, providing unparalleled scientific calculation capabilities to recommend increasingly reliable and efficient instruments.

In recent years, he has specialized in advanced quantitative analysis, financial programming, and predictive technologies, adopting a scientific approach to building efficient, risk-controlled portfolios with objective performance evaluation metrics.

Dr. Gobbis designs efficient portfolios leveraging professional software and artificial intelligence tools that apply advanced quantitative methodologies to optimize every investment stage. The analyses and algorithms rely on:

  • Real historical data,
  • Risk and return metrics,
  • Advanced optimization engines,

capable of translating theoretical simulations into coherent, dynamic, and personalized portfolio compositions.

Main application areas include:

  • Quantitative Asset Allocation and Efficient Frontier Construction
    Solutions are based on expected return and risk estimates for various asset classes, combined to identify optimal portfolios according to the principle of the best risk/volatility-adjusted return. The optimization engine enables the generation and periodic updating (e.g., every six months) of portfolios aligned with client risk profiles.
  • Historical Analyses and 36-Month Rolling Style Analysis
    Monthly data analyses on financial instruments and portfolios are conducted over 1-, 3-, and 5-year horizons. In particular, the 36-month rolling style analysis monitors the evolution of average fund composition and management activity over time.
  • Monte Carlo Simulations and Volatility Cone
    Using probabilistic forecasting tools like the volatility cone, potential fluctuations in expected returns over the investment horizon are represented, enabling clients to manage risk expectations more consciously.
  • Risk Budgeting and Multi-Objective Optimization
    Solutions are assessed with a multi-objective approach, considering not only returns but also stability, diversification, and portfolio compatibility. Optimization compares historical and current efficient frontiers, including flexible asset classes.
  • Financial Instrument Selection via Efficiency Indicators
    Tools like the Sharpe Ratio are employed to identify instruments that provide higher risk-adjusted returns compared to traditional static asset allocations.
  • Wealth Transfer Planning and Capital Protection
    The technical approach is integrated into a long-term strategic vision encompassing wealth protection, planned generational transfers, and safeguarding against unforeseen events.

In summary, Dr. Gobbis applies a rigorous, data-driven methodology integrating:

  • Historical and behavioral portfolio analyses;
  • Mathematical algorithms for efficient portfolio construction;
  • Performance indicators and risk management tools;
  • Continuous optimization tailored to individual profiles and goals.

The aim is to build measurable, replicable, and consistent financial strategies over time, delivering value both in the accumulation and preservation phases of wealth.

The solutions proposed are designed to ensure scalable, replicable, and measurable investment strategies focused on reducing uncertainty and optimizing the risk/return ratio over the long term.
